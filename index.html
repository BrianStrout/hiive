Title of Exhibit: Lisboa e Lonely

Location: Art Room, Principe Real, Lisbon Date: March 14, 15 and 16, 2024 Artists:
To be confirmed upon "signing on" below :)

Point of Contact:Brian Strout, reachable via instagram or whatsapp. Number will be posted here and shared with people in current conversations when we are all set up. Exhibit Concept:
Artists from the greater Lisbon area will be given two matching picture frames(supplied by curator) of the same size( 30cm x 40cm inner dim.) to create individual art based on how the artist interprets the title of the exhibit. Artists are encouraged to acknowledge the frame’s dimensions but not feel restricted by it. Frames have been ordered and are expected to arrive around the 26th of December.

Artwork made by artist
Altering of supplied frames is allowed providing at least three corners of the frame are left as supplied to uphold the homogeneous aesthetic. Of the 2 pieces Artists will supply to the show at least one should have an unaltered frame(again to help establish the aesthetic across the room/studio.)

The picture frames are black and have a thin wooden frame around them. The backing board is 30 x 40 cm and when placed in the frame 1/2 of a cm is covered by the frame so your displayed artwork if inside of the frame will actually sit at 29 x 39cm. You are not require to use the backer, and can leave out or replace if your piece needs something else or nothing in it's place.


The gallery kind enough to host us does have a commission for sales so they can also keep their lights on. Per the gallery's inhouse policy, this was determined to be 40%, of which 20% goes to the gallery and 20% to the currator.

Presentation
Art pieces would be unlabeled in the show, however there will be booklets available for guests upon arrival that would contain a key to the exhibit, listing prices, with a description of the effort, as well as artist bios(where they can introduce themselves briefly and help people in the show find your work elsewhere/online/etc.) I have quoted out proper books for the event and will update people on this in the next few weeks as I collect content from artists.

Photos for promotional content
Artists need to provide 2 photos, one of a straight forward “selfie” blank expression (think like a driver's license please) and also whatever they would like to be included with their bio. I will make promotional videos for advertising/sharing online as well as this page will turn into a landing page for the event online. I will share it under a new account hiivelisbon (on instagram) which I will be using for this and some other events down the line. In case you are curious the blank selfie is so I can match people faces easier while make promotional content. I would like to use it as a 9 square grid rubrics cube-ish transition. I don't expect ever actually see the whole photo. Artists should, I hope, be enthusiastic to help share this content to spread the word when it becomes available.

Opening Event Notes
We will be setting up a photobooth-like setting for the reception, and would happily take some help with this as the design comes together, if you are interested. Not required.

Food and Beverage:
The event currently will be sponsored by Musa! Exploring some other options

I'm happy to explore things with you but want to make sure we're not biting off more than we can chew.

Show Agreement

ARTROOM’s conditions for planned group show at our gallery.
  • 3 days exhibition format
  • Thursday- opening day
  • Friday and Saturday - viewing days
  • with 2 previous days for setting up and the immediate next day after the exhibition for dismantling it.
  • Besides the big exhibition room, we have one kitchen and a bathroom that can be used too.
  • Please note the artist studio can not be accessed at all!
  • Dates of exhibition: March 14, 15 and 16, 2024
  • Schedules: to be defined (usually from 5 to 8 or 6 to 9 pm
  • Please note: 9 pm maximum due to licenses)
  • Extra days for production: March 12, 13 and 17.
  • The artists can access and use the space only with the curator (Brian) supervision, during the schedule from 9 am to 9 pm (exceptionally other allowed persons can access it too during this period for production reasons).
  • The space must be delivered on Sunday March 17 at 9 pm max, at the exact same conditions as it was at the time the keys were handed on the set up day ( March 12, 9 am)
  • No parties allowed - with the exception of the open day until 9 pm - and no smoking inside (of any kind).
  • Please do not use our materials with no permission, a lot of artroom tools have been disappearing in the last years so each artist is responsible for bringing his own materials for setting up.
  • We kindly ask that all the remains of food, drinks and garbage are immediately removed from the gallery.
  • Considering it’s a building from the XIX century and for structural reasons, no dancing is possible nor very loud music for respect to our neighbours and Pavilhão Chinês the Bar bellow us. However it’s possible to have music, we always have :)
  • If the client asks for an invoice, which is not very frequent, is the artist responsibility to do it since it’s the artist that receives the total payment and pays the curator and the gallery commissions after. The gallery will invoice the value of its 20% commission to the artist if needed. The total sale will be shared the following way, 60% for the artist 20% for the curator and 20% for the gallery.


CURATOR Brian Strout GALLERIST Ana Araujo
